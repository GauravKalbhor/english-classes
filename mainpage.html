Present Indefinite Tenses

The simple present is a verb tense with two main uses. We use the simple present tense when an action is happening right now, or when it happens regularly (or unceasingly, which is why it’s sometimes called present indefinite). Depending on the person, the simple present tense is formed by using the root form or by adding ‑s or ‑es to the end.
In this we use Verb (v1) form.
Subject = I, you, we, they, he, she, it, anyname.
for 1st person to 2nd person

How to ASK Questions

In this first person ask a question to second person

  • DO you + V1
    • Yes I + V1
    • No I dont/do not + V1
    • No I never + V1
  1. DO you Action?
    • Yes I Action.
    • No I dont/do not Action.
    • No I never Action.
  2. DO you keep fast?
    • Yes I keep fast.
    • No I dont/do not keep fast.
    • No I never keep fast.
  3. DO you get up early in the morning?
    • Yes I get up early in the morning.
    • No I dont/do not get up early in the morning.
    • No I never get up early in the morning.
  4. DO you wear a healmet while rideing a bick?
    • Yes I wear a healmet while rideing a bick.
    • No I dont/do not wear a healmet while rideing a bick.
